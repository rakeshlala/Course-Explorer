B.Sc. (Hons) in Physics / Chemistry / Mathematics / Biology / etc.

1) What is B.Sc. (Hons)?

B.Sc. (Hons) stands for Bachelor of Science (Honours). It is an undergraduate degree focused on a specialized subject like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, etc. It offers more in-depth and research-oriented study compared to general B.Sc.

2) Key Features of B.Sc. (Hons)

Full Form

B.Sc. (Hons) — Bachelor of Science (Honours).

Duration

3 years (some may be 4 years in certain universities).

Eligibility

12th Science stream with specific subjects and usually at least 50% to 60% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • St. Stephen’s College, Delhi
  • Hindu College, Delhi University
  • Miranda House, Delhi
  • Loyola College, Chennai
  • Presidency College, Kolkata
  • Fergusson College, Pune
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU)
  • St. Xavier’s College, Mumbai

Admission

Based on merit (12th marks) or entrance exams, depending on the college.

Fees

₹15,000 – ₹60,000 per year in government colleges; ₹60,000 – ₹2,00,000 per year in private colleges.

Career Opportunities

  • Research Scientist
  • Lecturer/Professor (after M.Sc. & NET)
  • Scientific Assistant
  • Lab Supervisor
  • Data Analyst
  • Technical Specialist
  • Higher studies (M.Sc., PhD, etc.)

Salary in India

₹4 lakh – ₹6 lakh per year initially; can increase to ₹8 lakh – ₹12 lakh or more with experience and higher qualifications.

3) B.Sc. (Hons) Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th Science with required subject).
  • Step 2: Research and shortlist colleges/universities.
  • Step 3: Fill application form online/offline.
  • Step 4: Appear for entrance exam (if required).
  • Step 5: Check merit list or entrance results.
  • Step 6: Attend counseling and verify documents.
  • Step 7: Pay fees and confirm admission.

Summary

B.Sc. (Hons) is a specialized 3-year undergraduate degree for Science stream students, focusing deeply on one subject. It offers strong academic foundation, research exposure, and opens up higher study and research career options. Admissions are mostly merit-based or via entrance exams, with good job prospects and salary growth.
