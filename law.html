Integrated Law Courses (5 years)

1) What are Integrated Law Courses?

Integrated law courses combine a bachelor's degree with an LL.B., allowing students to complete both degrees in 5 years instead of doing them separately. These programs are designed for students who want to pursue a career in law directly after 12th.

2) Popular Integrated Law Degrees

Students can choose from:

  • B.A. LL.B. (Bachelor of Arts + Bachelor of Law)
  • BBA LL.B. (Bachelor of Business Administration + Bachelor of Law)

3) Key Features of Integrated Law Degrees

Full Form

Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Law (B.A. LL.B.) or Bachelor of Business Administration and Bachelor of Law (BBA LL.B.).

Duration

5 years (integrated course).

Eligibility

12th in any stream (Arts, Commerce, Science), usually with at least 45–50% marks. Admission often requires clearing entrance exams like CLAT, AILET, or state-level law entrance exams.

Top Colleges in India

  • National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bangalore
  • National Academy of Legal Studies and Research (NALSAR), Hyderabad
  • National Law University (NLU), Delhi
  • Symbiosis Law School, Pune
  • Jindal Global Law School, Sonipat

Admission

Based on entrance exams like CLAT, AILET, LSAT India, or institute-specific tests, followed by counseling and seat allocation.

Fees

₹1 lakh – ₹5 lakh per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Advocate/Lawyer
  • Legal Advisor
  • Corporate Legal Consultant
  • Judicial Services (after further exams)
  • Legal Analyst
  • Academician or Researcher

Salary in India

₹4 lakh – ₹10 lakh per year (starting); can be much higher in corporate law and top firms.

Summary

Integrated law courses are 5-year programs that help students build a strong foundation in law combined with arts or management subjects. These degrees prepare graduates for successful careers as advocates, legal consultants, and many other legal roles in India and abroad.
