B.Sc. in Nursing

1) What is B.Sc. in Nursing?

B.Sc. in Nursing is a professional undergraduate program that focuses on training students to become skilled nurses and healthcare providers. It covers subjects such as anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, nutrition, and advanced nursing care.

2) Key Features of B.Sc. in Nursing

Full Form

B.Sc. in Nursing — Bachelor of Science in Nursing.

Duration

4 years.

Eligibility

12th Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), usually with at least 45%–50% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Delhi
  • Christian Medical College (CMC), Vellore
  • Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC), Pune
  • Rajkumari Amrit Kaur College of Nursing, Delhi
  • PGIMER, Chandigarh
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi

Admission

Through entrance exams conducted by colleges/universities or based on 12th marks (depending on institute).

Fees

₹30,000 – ₹1,50,000 per year (government); ₹1,00,000 – ₹3,00,000 per year (private).

Career Opportunities

  • Staff Nurse
  • Nurse Educator
  • ICU Nurse
  • Nursing Superintendent
  • Research Nurse
  • Further studies (M.Sc. Nursing, etc.)

Salary in India

₹2.5 lakh – ₹5 lakh per year initially; can increase with experience and higher qualifications.

3) B.Sc. in Nursing Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th with PCB).
  • Step 2: Apply for entrance exams (if applicable).
  • Step 3: Appear for exam and qualify.
  • Step 4: Check merit list or counseling schedule.
  • Step 5: Attend counseling and verify documents.
  • Step 6: Pay admission fees and confirm seat.

Summary

B.Sc. in Nursing is a 4-year professional program that prepares students for a career in healthcare as registered nurses. It offers strong job security and opportunities for specialization or higher studies. Admission is through entrance exams or merit, with a focus on hands-on clinical training.
