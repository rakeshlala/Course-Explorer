B.Sc. in Environmental Science

1) What is B.Sc. in Environmental Science?

B.Sc. in Environmental Science is an undergraduate program focused on the study of environment, ecology, pollution control, and sustainable development. It prepares students to understand and solve environmental issues through scientific approaches.

2) Key Features of B.Sc. in Environmental Science

Full Form

B.Sc. in Environmental Science.

Duration

3 years.

Eligibility

12th Science stream, usually with at least 50% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • Fergusson College, Pune
  • Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), Delhi
  • University of Delhi
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi
  • St. Xavier’s College, Mumbai

Admission

Based on merit (12th marks) or entrance exams, depending on the university.

Fees

₹20,000 – ₹1,50,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Environmental Consultant
  • Environmental Scientist
  • Pollution Control Officer
  • Wildlife Conservationist
  • Waste Management Officer
  • Further studies (M.Sc., PhD, etc.)

Salary in India

₹3 lakh – ₹6 lakh per year (starting); can increase with experience and higher qualifications.

3) B.Sc. in Environmental Science Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th Science stream).
  • Step 2: Apply to colleges/universities.
  • Step 3: Appear for entrance exam (if required).
  • Step 4: Check merit list or exam result.
  • Step 5: Attend counseling and document verification.
  • Step 6: Pay fees and confirm admission.
