B.Sc. in Microbiology

1) What is B.Sc. in Microbiology?

B.Sc. in Microbiology is an undergraduate program focusing on the study of microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. It covers their roles in health, agriculture, industry, and the environment.

2) Key Features of B.Sc. in Microbiology

Full Form

B.Sc. in Microbiology — Bachelor of Science in Microbiology.

Duration

3 years.

Eligibility

12th Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), usually with at least 50% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • St. Xavier’s College, Mumbai
  • Fergusson College, Pune
  • Madras Christian College, Chennai
  • Panjab University, Chandigarh
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi

Admission

Mostly merit-based (12th marks) or through entrance exams in some universities.

Fees

₹30,000 – ₹1,50,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Microbiologist
  • Research Assistant
  • Quality Control Executive
  • Food Safety Officer
  • Clinical Lab Technician
  • Further studies (M.Sc., PhD, etc.)

Salary in India

₹3 lakh – ₹6 lakh per year (starting); increases with experience and higher qualifications.

3) B.Sc. in Microbiology Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th with PCB).
  • Step 2: Apply to preferred colleges/universities.
  • Step 3: Appear for entrance exam (if required).
  • Step 4: Check merit list or exam results.
  • Step 5: Attend counseling and document verification.
  • Step 6: Pay fees and confirm admission.
