B.Sc. in Forensic Science

1) What is B.Sc. in Forensic Science?

B.Sc. in Forensic Science is an undergraduate program focused on scientific techniques used in crime investigation and legal processes. It involves studying criminalistics, toxicology, ballistics, fingerprint analysis, and more to help solve crimes.

2) Key Features of B.Sc. in Forensic Science

Full Form

B.Sc. in Forensic Science.

Duration

3 years.

Eligibility

12th Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics), usually with at least 50% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • Amity University, Noida
  • Jain University, Bangalore
  • University of Mumbai
  • Osmania University, Hyderabad
  • Gujarat Forensic Sciences University

Admission

Through entrance exams or based on 12th marks, depending on the university.

Fees

₹50,000 – ₹2,00,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Forensic Scientist
  • Crime Scene Investigator
  • DNA Analyst
  • Fingerprint Expert
  • Forensic Toxicologist
  • Further studies (M.Sc., PhD, etc.)

Salary in India

₹3 lakh – ₹6 lakh per year (starting); increases with experience and specialization.

Summary

B.Sc. in Forensic Science is a 3-year program that trains students to apply scientific methods to solve crimes and support the legal system. It offers strong career prospects in government and private crime labs, as well as opportunities for higher studies and specialization.
