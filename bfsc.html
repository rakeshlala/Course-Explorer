B.F.Sc (Bachelor of Fisheries Science)

1) What is B.F.Sc?

B.F.Sc (Bachelor of Fisheries Science) is an undergraduate program focused on the study of fisheries, aquaculture, fish processing, and marine biology. It aims to train students in scientific breeding, conservation, and management of fish and aquatic resources.

2) Key Features of B.F.Sc

Full Form

Bachelor of Fisheries Science.

Duration

4 years.

Eligibility

12th Science stream (Biology/Agriculture), usually with at least 50% aggregate marks.

Top Colleges in India

  • Central Institute of Fisheries Education, Mumbai
  • College of Fisheries, Mangalore
  • Kerala University of Fisheries and Ocean Studies, Kochi
  • College of Fisheries, Ratnagiri
  • Tamil Nadu Dr. J. Jayalalithaa Fisheries University, Nagapattinam

Admission

Based on entrance exams or merit (12th marks), depending on the college/university.

Fees

₹20,000 – ₹1,50,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Fisheries Officer
  • Aquaculture Manager
  • Fish Breeder
  • Marine Biologist
  • Fish Processing Plant Manager
  • Further studies (M.F.Sc, research, etc.)

Salary in India

₹3 lakh – ₹6 lakh per year (starting); increases with experience and specialization.

3) B.F.Sc Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th with Biology/Agriculture).
  • Step 2: Apply to colleges/universities.
  • Step 3: Appear for entrance exam (if required).
  • Step 4: Check merit list or exam result.
  • Step 5: Attend counseling and document verification.
  • Step 6: Pay fees and confirm admission.
