BFIA (Bachelor of Financial and Investment Analysis)

1) What is BFIA?

BFIA (Bachelor of Financial and Investment Analysis) is a specialized 3-year undergraduate program that focuses on finance, investment strategies, financial analysis, portfolio management, and related business subjects. It is designed for students aiming for careers in finance, investment banking, and asset management.

2) Key Features of BFIA

Full Form

Bachelor of Financial and Investment Analysis.

Duration

3 years.

Eligibility

12th in any stream (preferably Commerce or with Mathematics), usually with at least 50–60% aggregate marks. Some universities require Mathematics as a mandatory subject.

Top Colleges in India

  • Shaheed Sukhdev College of Business Studies (SSCBS), Delhi University (main college offering BFIA)

Admission

Based on entrance exams (like DU JAT) and 12th merit depending on the university's admission policy.

Fees

₹50,000 – ₹1,50,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • Financial Analyst
  • Investment Banker
  • Portfolio Manager
  • Wealth Manager
  • Equity Research Analyst
  • Further studies (MBA, CFA, etc.)

Salary in India

₹4 lakh – ₹10 lakh per year (starting); higher salaries possible in top investment firms and banks.

3) BFIA Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility (12th passed, preferably with Mathematics).
  • Step 2: Apply to colleges/universities offering BFIA.
  • Step 3: Appear for entrance exam (e.g., DU JAT for Delhi University).
  • Step 4: Check merit list or exam result.
  • Step 5: Attend counseling and document verification.
  • Step 6: Pay fees and confirm admission.

Summary

BFIA is a 3-year specialized undergraduate program focused on finance and investment analysis. It is an ideal choice for students interested in making a career in financial services, investment banking, and asset management, and provides a strong foundation for further studies like MBA or CFA.
