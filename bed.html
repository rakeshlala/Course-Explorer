B.Ed. (Bachelor of Education)

1) What is B.Ed.?

B.Ed. (Bachelor of Education) is a 2-year professional undergraduate program designed to train students for careers in teaching and education. It provides knowledge of teaching methods, educational psychology, curriculum development, and classroom management.

2) Key Features of B.Ed.

Full Form

Bachelor of Education.

Duration

2 years.

Eligibility

Bachelor’s degree in any stream (Arts, Commerce, Science) with at least 50% marks. Some colleges may have entrance exams like CUCET, DU B.Ed. Entrance, or state-level exams.

Top Colleges in India

  • Lady Shri Ram College for Women, Delhi
  • Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi
  • Delhi University (DU), Delhi
  • Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Mumbai

Admission

Based on entrance exams or merit, followed by counseling and document verification.

Fees

₹20,000 – ₹1,00,000 per year (varies by college).

Career Opportunities

  • School Teacher (Primary or Secondary)
  • Educational Counselor
  • Academic Coordinator
  • Curriculum Developer
  • Educational Administrator
  • Further studies (M.Ed., Ph.D., etc.)

Salary in India

₹2 lakh – ₹5 lakh per year (starting); higher salaries in private schools or government positions with experience.

3) B.Ed. Admission Process (Steps)

  • Step 1: Check eligibility and choose college/university.
  • Step 2: Apply for entrance exam (if required) or submit merit-based application.
  • Step 3: Appear for entrance exam and check results.
  • Step 4: Attend counseling and document verification.
  • Step 5: Pay fees and confirm admission.

Summary

B.Ed. is a 2-year professional course that equips graduates with teaching skills and educational knowledge, making them eligible to work as teachers in schools or pursue higher studies in education and research.
